Wait Time Distributions

Our analysis reveals a significant "long tail" in patient wait times. While the median wait is manageable, a substantial number of patients experience delays exceeding 45 minutes before seeing a physician.

Figure 1: Distribution of key time metrics across all visits.

Triage System Effectiveness

The triage protocol is functioning as intended. Critical patients are successfully prioritized, bypassing the standard queues. However, this comes at the cost of significantly higher delays for "Medium" and "Low" priority cases.

Figure 2: Boxplot comparing doctor wait times by triage level. Note the tight distribution for Critical cases.

Resource Allocation & Doctor Load

There is a notable imbalance in doctor utilization. Top performers (e.g., D016, D004) are handling over 260 patients, while others handle significantly fewer. This variance suggests an opportunity for better shift scheduling.

Figure 3: Doctor efficiency map. Larger bubbles indicate longer average patient wait times for that doctor.

Strategic Recommendations

1. Redistribute High-Volume Shifts

Data indicates burnout risk for top 5 doctors. We recommend implementing a "Float Pool" of physicians to cover peak hours for these high-volume providers.

2. Implement "Fast Track" Lane

Since 40% of patients are Low/Medium acuity but wait >30 mins, a Nurse Practitioner-led Fast Track lane could reduce physician load by ~25%.

3. Flex-Staffing for Registration

Arrival spikes at 9 AM and 6 PM correlate with registration bottlenecks. Adding 2 part-time input staff during these windows will smooth the initial flow.
